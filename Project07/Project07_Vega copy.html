Directed by: Edward Zwick

Written by: John Logan


Tom Cruise, Ken Watanabe, Masato Harada

Released December 5, 2003


Tom Cruise (Nathan Algren): Playing the main role of this film, after his exceptional history in movie acting as one of the top 100 best actors here he performes as a alcoholic and self conflicted mercenary infantry captain sent on a mission to train the then time Japanese Imperial Army, captured in battle he finds himself and regain his honor as a Samurai for past sins.


Ken Watanabe (Katsumoto): Samurai leader, and warior-poet who is rebeling against the modernising Imperial Army. Bent on keeping Japan traditional by guiding his student, Emperor Meiji, he also seeks to guide Captain Algren in his quest for "bushido".

Masato Harada (Omura): Emperor Meijis Right hand and top advisor in the Japanese Imperial Council. His plan on diluting Japans traditions for a more western ideal take effect on the country and Samurai as he threatends their further need for the Empire.


Trailer provided by Warner Bros.


Synopsis: Japan 1870s, The Last Samurai tells the story of Captain Nathan Algren (Tom Cruise), a self-conflicted American military officer hired by the Emperor of Japan to train the country's first army in the art of modern warfare. As the Emperor and his advisors attempt to eradicate the ancient Samurai warriors in preparation for more Westernized government, Captain Algren becomes influenced by his exchanges in captivity with the Samurai, placing him in a struggling position on where his regaining honor should lie.
